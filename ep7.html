Justice and Integrity

Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) personally carried food to a needy family, emphasizing personal responsibility and justice. His humility and commitment to fairness continue to inspire as a model of leadership.

Story

Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) was renowned for his commitment to justice, fairness, and accountability. One notable instance during his caliphate illustrates this: Umar (RA) learned about a family in need and personally carried food on his back to provide for them. When questioned about why he did not delegate the task, he responded, “I will be questioned about them on the Day of Judgment.” This story highlights the importance of taking personal responsibility and ensuring justice, regardless of one’s status or position. Umar’s (RA) humility and fairness made him a model leader whose legacy continues to inspire.

Reflection

Justice and integrity are central to living a life that pleases Allah. Umar’s (RA) actions prompt us to ask: Are we fair and just in our dealings with others? Do we take responsibility for those who depend on us? Justice begins with small, everyday actions and extends to our roles within our families, workplaces, and communities.

Application

Act Justly: Identify an area in your life where you can improve fairness. This might be resolving a conflict impartially, treating colleagues or family members equitably, or ensuring transparency in your dealings.

Take Responsibility: Reflect on the needs of those around you and take tangible steps to address them. This could include helping a neighbor, mentoring someone in need, or supporting a charitable cause.
